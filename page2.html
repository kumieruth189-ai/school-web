Kento Yamazaki is a popular Japanese actor, born on September 7, 1994, in Itabashi, Tokyo, Japan. He first gained attention in the entertainment industry after his debut in 2010, but it was his breakout role in 2011's Seventeen magazine that really launched his career. Over the years, Yamazaki has become one of Japan's most well-known young actors, starring in a variety of both television dramas and films.

His most notable works include roles in adaptations of popular manga and anime. He played the lead in live-action adaptations such as Your Eyes Tell (2020), L-DK (2014), and Death Note: Light Up the New World (2016). One of his biggest roles came with his portrayal of the character of Ryota in the live-action adaptation of Kimi no Todoke (2010), where he played alongside actress Nao Matsushita.

Yamazaki's appeal comes not only from his good looks and charismatic on-screen presence, but also from his versatility in different genres. He is known for his ability to shift from romantic roles to more action-oriented or dramatic performances. Over the years, his career has gained him a large and loyal fan base in Japan and abroad.
